----------DOMINATION----------

 

Developed by: Ayush Mehra and Vincent Yang

 

Description:

 

Domination is a two-player strategy game. Each player has his own territory on either side of a grid-like battlefield. At the start of the game both players receive a certain amount of money, in the form of coins, which they can spend in the store to purchase several kinds of monsters(offense), obstacles such as walls(defense), or items like health packs(power-ups). Once bought, these items are stored in each playerÕs inventory and can be placed strategically in a live battlefield. Monsters will attempt to reach the other side of the battlegrid, fighting off opposing monsters and overcoming obstacles, and attack the opposing playerÕs territory and decrease his health. When one playerÕs health completely diminishes, the game ends.

 

Rules and Restrictions:

Monsters can only be placed in the first column of the grid, meaning they must start at either edge of the battlegrid. Each monster has its own health, during fights, both monsters reduce each others health(some faster than others) until one monster has zero health, at which point he dies. Monsters do not change rows. Unlike monsters, players can place obstacles only in their half of the battlegrid in empty locations. These obstacles only last for a limited time, each with its own characteristics. Players gain money when one of their monsters wins a fight and when one of their monsters successfully reaches the opposing sideÕs territory. Items from the store can be bought and placed while the battlegrid is in action. Your own obstacles do not affect your monsters.

 

Domination will be played mostly be kids and teens as a leisurely activity while utilizing appropriate strategies.

Instructions:

 

On the main menu, click on ÔPlayÕ. On the new screen, enter the name of each player in the appropriate text fields and click on ÔPlayÕ. You will now see the main game screen. In the center there will be a big 3D grid, with blue gridlines on the left half and red gridlines on the right half. On either side of the screen you will see a panel with the playerÕs info (name, health, money, and inventory) and the store(same for both players). In the store you will see three numbered options: Monsters, Obstacles, and Powerups. Player one will use the numbers at the top of the keyboard and player two will use the keypad to select one of the numbered options. Once an option is selected, the store will be updated with items of that category, again numbered. To purchase an item press the appropriate number key in the same procedure as before or press the number for the ÔMain StoreÕ key to return to the main store. After purchasing either a monster or obstacle, you will see your inventory update. After purchasing a power-up, your health will immediately update. Throughout the game you will always see a marker in a cell in your half of the battlegrid. Player one can move this marker around his side of the grid using the w,a,s,d keys while player two does this using the arrow keys. When the marker is in the desired cell, player one will press the space bar and player two will press enter. This action will update the playerÕs panel with a list of items from the playerÕs inventory that can be placed in that location. Remember, monsters can only be placed in the first column and obstacles can only be placed in the playerÕs half of the battlegrid in empty locations. Like before for purchasing items, press the appropriate number key and the item will be added to that location, if possible. Or press space bar(player one) or enter(player two) to cancel placement.

Tips:

Strategize! Plan where and when to place items on the grid appropriately for best results.

 

 

Interfaces:

Item (ability to be placed on the battlegrid)

Movable (ability to move)

Health (has a measurable health)

 

Classes:

Domination (main JFrame; contains main method) 

BattleGrid (represents the battlegrid in a 2D array)

Monster (implements Item, Movable, and Health; the most basic monster)

         BoltMonster (extends Monster; moves more quickly but is weaker)

         BuffMonster (extendsMonster; moves more slowly but is much stronger)

SpittingMonster (extends Monster; moves just like Monster but can attack on a longer range)

JuggernautMonster (extends Monster; faster and stronger than normal monster)

Wall (implements Item and Health; obstacle; decreases health of contacting monster)

         StickyWall (extends Wall; decreases speed of contacting monster)

         StallWall (extends Wall; stops contacting monster for a period of time)

         FireWall (extends Wall; decreases health of contacting monster more drastically)

Powerup (increases playerÕs health)

         FirstAid (method in Powerup; increases playerÕs health by a small amount)

         Hospital (method in Powerup; increases playerÕs health drastically)

         Reincarnation(method in Powerup; sets playerÕs health to 100 percent)

Player (implements KeyListener;  stores and displayÕs a playerÕs information, health, inventory, and store in a panel)

Store (represents the store and maintains list of all items that can be purchased)
