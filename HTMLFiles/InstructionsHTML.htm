From the home screen click ‘Play’.

 

You will now see the playerNames screen.

On this screen, enter in the names of the two players. If left empty, default names

will be assigned. When ready press ‘Play’.

 

Now, on the screen, there will be a big 3D grid, with neon blue gridlines on the left half and red gridlines on the right half. On either side of the bottom of the screen you will see a panel with the player’s info (name, health, and money) and the store(same for both players).

 

Throughout the game you will always see a marker in a cell in your

half of the battlegrid. Player one can move this marker around his side of the grid using

the W,A,S,D keys while player two does this using the arrow keys. Your marker can only be moved in your half of the grid. When the marker is in the desired cell, player one can buy affordable items through the store.

 

In the store you will see eight numbered options of different types of Monsters, Obstacles, and Powerups. Player one will use the numbers at the top of the keyboard and player two will use the keypad to select one of the numbered options. To purchase an item press the appropriate number. If affordable, after purchasing a monster it will appear in the first column of your side of the grid and all obstacles will appear in the current marker location(if empty). Powerups can only be purchased if health is your health is not currently 100%. After purchasing a power-up, a visual cue will appear on your side of the screen and your health will immediately update.

 

Both players start with 2000 coins and every 30 seconds, both players gain 500 more coins. The objective is to get as many monsters to the other side of the grid by placing them strategically on the board. Every time a monster reaches the other side, it will remove itself from the battlegrid and the opponent’s health will decrease by 5%. Ostacles add another twist to the game as they each play different roles. For more information about specific monsters and obstacles, look below.

 

Tips:

Strategize! Plan where and when to place items on the grid appropriately for best

results.
